Selection of Safe & Effective Antimicrobial Therapy

Aim — To select the safest, most effective and most appropriate antimicrobial for a patient using the infection, culture & sensitivity (antibiogram), allergy, kidney function and pregnancy status, applying antimicrobial-stewardship principles. New to antimicrobial selection? Start here

Choosing an antibiotic is not just "which one kills the bug". A good choice must be Safe, Effective, and appropriate (stewardship). Work through each case like this:

  1. Identify the infection & likely organism — use the site of infection and any culture & sensitivity (antibiogram) result.
  2. Effective? — the antibiotic must cover the organism and be sensitive on the antibiogram (not resistant).
  3. Safe? — check allergy, pregnancy and kidney function. Never choose a drug the patient is allergic to or that is contraindicated.
  4. Stewardship — pick the narrowest effective agent, the right route (oral if possible), and de-escalate once cultures are back. Save broad-spectrum drugs for when they are truly needed.

Common safety rules to remember

SituationAvoid / caution
Penicillin allergyAvoid penicillins & co-amoxiclav; use a macrolide, doxycycline (caution with cephalosporins)
PregnancyAvoid fluoroquinolones (ciprofloxacin) & tetracyclines (doxycycline); trimethoprim in 1st trimester
Poor kidney functionAvoid nitrofurantoin (eGFR < 45); dose-adjust or avoid nephrotoxic agents (e.g. gentamicin)
StewardshipUse the narrowest effective agent; de-escalate from broad-spectrum once cultures return
